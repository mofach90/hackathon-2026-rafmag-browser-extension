# 01 — Problem

> Goal of this doc: name the pain. What's broken in the world today, who feels it, and why it's worth solving now.

## Pain points

- The `rafmag` radio show is broadcast live on YouTube. After the live ends, the **unedited recording** stays available on the channel under YouTube's "Live" section.
- Listeners often play that recording later as **background audio while doing other tasks** (chores, tidying up, cooking).
- The recording includes the **moderator's breaks** — long stretches where the host steps away and the radio fills the airtime with **loud, intrusive promos** for upcoming shows.
- Because the listener is away from the computer doing other things, the only way to skip those segments today is to **physically walk back, find the right place in the timeline, and seek past the promo** — every single time it happens.
- This breaks the experience the listener actually wants: a passive, uninterrupted re-listen of the show in the background.

## Why now

- The show already exists, the recordings already exist, the audience already exists.
- The friction is real, recurring (every episode), and per-episode it happens multiple times.
- It's a perfect hackathon-scale problem: well-defined, single user flow, browser-only — no backend strictly required.

## What happens if nobody solves this

- Listeners keep being yanked away from whatever they were doing to manually skip ads.
- Some give up and stop listening to the archived recordings altogether — they lose access to a show they enjoy in the form they prefer (background, on-demand).
