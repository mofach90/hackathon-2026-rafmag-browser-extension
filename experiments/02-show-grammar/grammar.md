## 5. False-positive traps

**Trap 1: Live In-Show Sponsor Reads (e.g., Ooredoo, Volkswagen)**
- **Why a naive matcher would flag it:** The transcripts contain heavy promotional language, jingles, and brand names (e.g., "مع اوريدو جات اللحظه", "بونداشا قيمته 500 دينار للربح من اهداء فولكسفاجن").
- **Why it isn't a break:** These are live sponsor reads and giveaways conducted by the panel *during* the show. The moderator talks to callers and gives away prizes. The main show has not paused.
- **Examples:** 
  - EPISODE 0U5ek6-25ZA at [1015]: ">> مع اوريدو جات اللحظه باش انت تقول علقت..."
  - EPISODE ATgHQZDqHEY at [2277]: "نذكركم بالحدث ونذكركم بالربح و نذكركم بال 500 دينار بونداشال الربح كل يوم... فولكسفاجن تودكم..."

**Trap 2: Station News & Sports Flashes (موجز الأخبار / فلاش سبور)**
- **Why a naive matcher would flag it:** A human voice is reading the news or sports clearly and professionally, which looks like normal talk-radio content.
- **Why it isn't a break:** Based on the calibration example, the Diwan FM generic station news (read by a different anchor, e.g., "مجزوه ثامنه ديوان افم") is considered *part of the noisy radio break*. The "show content" strictly refers to the Raf Mag panel.
- **Examples:**
  - EPISODE ATgHQZDqHEY at [3702]: ">> مجزوه ثامنه ديوان افم صباح الخير"
  - EPISODE 0U5ek6-25ZA at [3087]: ">> صباح النور مرحبا بكم في الفلاش سبور"

**Trap 3: Zapping Replays**
- **Why a naive matcher would flag it:** The audio plays the moderator saying "فاصل" (break).
- **Why it isn't a break:** It is an audio montage of funny moments from previous days played during the live show. 
- **Examples:** 
  - EPISODE 0U5ek6-25ZA at [380]: "تو عملنا فاصل باش تعمل شكون مات هذك" (Spoken during the Zapping montage).


## 6. Confidence and caveats
- **Usable Transcripts:** The transcripts for all provided episodes (0U5ek6-25ZA, ATgHQZDqHEY, C5f12KL93Mk, NkHZdVwQQRM, OMQ47PuT04I, r8XrQLQ5TYQ, mhbW1DIq1Q0, h3n2BLlotEM, IwY1p4vO3M4) are highly usable for structural detection. Despite the heavy dialectal noise, the automated promotional blocks (e.g., "في البرمجه الشتويه على ديوان اف ام... عيش البوزيتيف") are repeated verbatim during every single break. This makes programmatic detection of the break blocks extremely reliable.
- **Uncertainty around exact boundaries:** The exact timestamp of when a break ends is slightly ambiguous because Diwan FM plays an automated station ID just before handing back to the live studio ("تسمعوا الراف ماك من اثنين للجمعه من السبعه للتسعه ونص مع رفيق بشناق"). A detector will need to decide whether to cut at the beginning or the end of this specific pre-recorded bumper.
- **Dialectal variations in spelling:** Because the transcripts are auto-generated from Tunisian Derja, words like "Zapping" can be spelled as "زبينغ", "ذابينج", or "زبينيك". "Chroniqueur" can be "كرونيكور". Any text-matching regex must account for phonetic Arabic spelling variations.
